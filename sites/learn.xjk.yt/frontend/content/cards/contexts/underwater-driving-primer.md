# Underwater Driving Primer

Underwater driving is not just normal driving with less speed. The car often behaves as if it is lighter, less planted, and more willing to keep unusual contact states.

This card exists as shared context. A more specific card should not need to re-explain every underwater assumption before it teaches one trick.

## What changes underwater

- Speed loss and grip do not always match what the surface suggests above water.
- Two-wheel or one-wheel contact can be useful instead of instantly being a mistake.
- Slopes, edges, and block seams can change whether the car feels planted or floaty.
- Some cars keep orientation unusually well, which can turn awkward jumps into controlled tools.

::tip{title="How to read underwater cards"}
Treat underwater techniques as contact-state problems. Ask what the wheels are touching, what the car is trying to align with, and whether the technique is preserving speed or just preserving control.
::

## Shared vocabulary

- **Contact state**: how many wheels are touching useful ground, wall, or edge.
- **Floatiness**: the car staying light enough that normal grip assumptions become unreliable.
- **Speed retention**: keeping speed that the surface would normally remove.
- **Field note**: a player observation that is useful but may still need better tests.
