# Floatiness, Slopes & Flowtrick

Rally underwater is floaty enough that partial contact can be useful. A car with two wheels on a block and two wheels off the block may keep more speed than a fully planted line on a penalty surface.

This can also happen naturally on uphills and downhills.

## Slopes

Field notes describe Rally underwater as not losing speed on uphills, while downhills make grip easier to lose. For downhill turns, a clean noslide line can drag the car too far outside because the grip is weaker.

The practical adjustment:

1. Enter slightly more inside than normal.
2. Accept a small oversteer if it protects the line.
3. Prioritize the exit contact state over the prettiest-looking turn.

## Partial contact

Partial wheel contact matters because it can bypass some surface penalties. The car is not simply "off the block"; it can be in a useful mixed state.

Useful questions:

- How many wheels are touching?
- Is the car supported enough to steer?
- Is the floating side helping speed or destroying control?

## Flowtrick

When returning from two-wheel contact to four-wheel contact, the car can slow down. Pholtur describes a flowtrick that shifts the car's weight so one side lifts slightly, helping the car return without the same slowdown.

This needs clips before it becomes a strict step-by-step guide, but the concept belongs here: underwater floatiness can be steered into a weight shift instead of merely endured.
