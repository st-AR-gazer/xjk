# Reactors, Edges & Overwalls

Rally underwater stays stable enough that special forces and block edges can become deliberate tools instead of random chaos.

## Reactors

Reactor down behaves broadly like above water.

Reactor up depends on color:

- Green reactor up can drive along the ground with low grip.
- Orange reactor up immediately sends the car upward.
- Rally stability gives useful control during long orange reactor flights.

Field notes suggest precise jumps across very large distances are possible. The risk is landing straight down from great height: all four wheels can get stuck in the ground, leaving the car unable to drive.

::callout{type="warning" title="Possible effect-block use"}
The wheel-stuck landing might be useful for getting an effect block underground, but this is a hypothesis, not a guide yet.
::

## Edges and overwalls

Rally underwater can bounce from many block edges and corners to about a block's height even at around 100 speed. That can allow pool exits if the pool has an inward-facing corner piece.

It can also overwall many objects:

- road borders
- castle borders
- cacti
- low penalty scenery layers

Backward overwalling appears stronger than forward overwalling.

## One-wheel wallclimb

On blocks with wooden sides, Rally underwater can perform a one-wheel wallclimb. With a yellow booster, field notes suggest it can climb over a full block height.

## Plastic edge oddity

If a plastic platform is rotated 90 degrees for a wallride, the top surface becomes rubber. But driving perfectly on the edge can make the car behave as if it still has plastic physics while on top of the rubber.

This is exactly the kind of mechanic Learn should preserve as a card: tiny, weird, and very hard to rediscover from scratch.
