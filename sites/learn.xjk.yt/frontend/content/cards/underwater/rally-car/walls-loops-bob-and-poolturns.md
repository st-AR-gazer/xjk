# Walls, Loops, Bob & Poolturns

Rally underwater can technically drive wallrides, but normal wallride thinking is dangerous. Field notes say the car continuously loses speed on wallrides, can come to a halt, and then slide downward while feeling glued to the wall.

The useful wall mechanic is often not staying on the wall. It is leaving the wall at the right moment.

## Wallride exit jump

While entering a wallride, a sharp downward steering input can jump the car out of the wallride. This can create extra height and distance away from the wall, making some quarterpipe transitions reachable.

## Loops

Loops have similar problems. Rally underwater may drop roof-down from the top of the loop.

Possible solutions from field notes:

- Drive diagonal lines through the loop to keep speed.
- Wiggle at the top to avoid falling out.
- Turn sideways before falling out so the car enters a wallride-like slide instead of dropping onto the roof.

## Bob

Normal bob lines do not transfer cleanly underwater.

At high speeds from boosters or reactors, a wallride-like line may be the only way to keep speed through the bob. Around normal 100 speed, options depend on the sharpness of the bob:

- noslide the whole turn
- noslide until the apex, quick ice slide, then noslide again
- ice slide the whole inside wall with one wheel stable below the yellow line

## Poolturns

Rally underwater can wallhug the inside of poolturns while keeping full speed. This is a good example of why Rally underwater should be mapped through block geometry and contact, not only through vehicle category.
