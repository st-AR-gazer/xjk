# Air Control & Ledge Grabs

Rally underwater has a form of air control. If you steer before jumping forward, the car can keep turning in that direction while you hold the steering input. Countersteering can cancel it.

Backward jumps are different: field notes say you need to release brake and press gas before leaving the block if you want usable air turning.

## Ground-parallel behavior

The Rally car tries to stay parallel to the ground while airborne. Underwater, that stability creates useful tricks.

On a quarterpipe or steep upward launch:

1. The nose naturally wants to rotate from upward-facing toward downward-facing.
2. Precise steering can resist that rotation.
3. If the car reaches the top with the nose held up, it can re-align parallel to the ground.
4. That can let the car "grab" ledges behind the quarterpipe that would otherwise be out of reach.

The Snowcar underwater spinlift trick also appears to work with Rally underwater, but with less height gain. It has reportedly already been needed for at least one Altered Nadeo validation.

That makes this card a cross-link between Rally Underwater, Snowcar Underwater, and Kacky-style transition logic.
