# Start Trick & Penalty Wiggles

The recurring input idea is simple: Rally underwater can sometimes accelerate or retain speed better when steering is not static. The exact rhythm is still a testing topic, but multiple DIVE notes point at steering rhythm being a major mechanic.

## Start trick

On flat start blocks, a quick left-right steering input can give faster acceleration. Notes from Mulga and Patriam suggest this can matter on dirt, grass, and even road, with controller and keyboard attempts feeling different.

Known shape:

1. Begin with a small steering movement instead of a dead-straight start.
2. Make the correction early enough that it affects acceleration, not just line.
3. Keep the car from turning so much that the gain is spent on a worse approach.

::tip{title="Testing note"}
The start trick may depend heavily on input device and steering curve. Learn should eventually store ghost clips or input traces beside this card.
::

## Penalty wiggles

Penalty surfaces normally try to pull Rally underwater toward lower speed. Field notes suggest steering rhythm can reduce or bypass that loss.

- Sand can feel close to no speed loss with strong wiggles.
- Penalty grass appears to lose speed more slowly, often trending toward the penalty limit.
- Snow behavior is disputed in older notes: one test said it did not work, later notes said it can work but loses speed faster.
- Backward Rally may gain speed with wiggles in some penalty contexts, even below the expected limit.

::callout{type="warning" title="Do not overclaim this yet"}
The card should say "appears", "field notes suggest", and "needs repeatable tests" until we have controlled maps, clips, and input traces.
::

## Why it matters

If the wiggle works, a penalty surface stops being a hard stop and becomes a route-design question. That affects cuts, underwater routing, and any pack that tries to answer "how do I keep speed on penalty?"
