# Surfaces & Speed Retention

Rally underwater surface behavior is a mix of familiar driving and strange exceptions. The useful default is:

> Drive normal surfaces inside and clean. Watch for contact states and special surfaces that break the default.

## Normal surfaces

Road, dirt, grass, and wood are close enough that a first-pass route usually focuses on clean noslide turns. The player tries to stay inside, avoid excess slide, and protect exit speed.

That does not mean the surfaces are identical. It means the same simple technique often works until a block shape or penalty rule changes the problem.

## Ice and plastic

Ice does not have enough grip for strong noslide turns, so Rally underwater can use ice-slide-like behavior.

Plastic is one of the strongest Rally underwater surfaces:

- acceleration is strong
- small oversteer can still keep around 100 speed
- plastic downhills can break the usual 100-speed ceiling
- overspeed from boosters or reactors appears to last longer on plastic than on most surfaces

::callout{type="warning" title="Plastic is route-defining"}
If a route has plastic underwater, treat it as a speed storage surface, not just another texture.
::

## Penalty surfaces

Penalty surfaces technically limit speed, but Rally underwater can work around the limit in two broad ways:

1. Use partial contact, such as two wheels on the block and two wheels floating.
2. Use steering rhythm / AK-style wiggles to reduce speed loss.

That makes penalty behavior a shared topic between Surfaces, Inputs, and Grip & Contact. A route answer about penalty should probably link both this card and [[underwater/rally-car/start-trick-and-penalty-wiggles]].
